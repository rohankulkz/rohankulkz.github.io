Turning a Bedroom into a Smart Club

These lights are modeled after strobe style lighting at concerts

The setup uses an Trione bluetooth reciever, addressable RGB lights, and a custom software that sends Bluetooth signals to control brightness, color, and effects.

Tech Stack

  • Trione bluetooth reciever
  • LED strips
  • Laptop

System Design

Here's a high level system design. FFT analyzes audio output from a synthetic audio device called blackhole which is installed on the computer. Then, a frequency spectra is made to extract the compotent amplitudes of every frequency within the sound wave. Then, predictive models as well as other visualization tools are applied to the spectra and light sequences are generated (think, deep bass -> strobing, drops -> bright flashes, etc). These commands are then transmitted via an async thread to the bluetooth reciever, which displays the light sequences

Challenges

Bluetooth stability was the trickiest part. I spent days debugging unexpected disconnects, delays, and other interference. Eventually, I optimized packet sizes and update frequencies and added retry logic to get reliable performance.
